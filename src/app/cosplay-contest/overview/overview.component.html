Cosplay Contest Overview

The ColossalCon Cruise 2026 cosplay contests brings two distinct experiences to the high seas. Con goers are invited to participate in the laid-back "It's the Beach Episode" contest held on the sunny shores of CocoCay, and the formal "Tournament Arc" contest hosted aboard the cruise ship. Each contest is unique, tailored to different cosplay styles and preferences detailed below.

Cosplay Contest Experiences

It's the Beach Episode
Friday, February 13th, 2026 at CocoCay
This cosplay contest embraces our most beloved beach episodes
  • Begins @ 10am. Be early!
  • This contest has a more relaxed and fun atmosphere. It will take place directly on the beach at CocoCay.
  • The Craftsmanship category will NOT be divided into divisions
  • Costumes must be beach themed and swimwear-appropriate. Any costume that does not meet this requirement will be ineligible to participate.
The Tournament Arc
Sunday, February 15th, 2026 at the Star Lounge

The more traditional, structured cosplay competition. Hosted in the Star Lounge onboard the cruise ship, designed for full-scale costumes and technical evaluation.

  • Begins @ 1:30pm. Be early!
  • This is our more traditional and structured cosplay contest. It will be hosted in the Star Lounge on the cruise ship.
  • The Craftsmanship category will be divided into 3 divisions, based on the contestants skill level.
  • Costumes must be traditional, full-scale costume. To ensure separation of contests, swimwear will not be accepted in this contest.
Any contestant may enter both contests, provided they meet the costume requirements for each.
Any contestant not meeting the costume requirements for either contest will be disqualified from that specific contest.

Showcase Category Descriptions & Entry Criteria

Runway

  • This is a fashion show designed to showcase your costume and/or character performance to the audience.
  • Contestants will be judged based on performance and presentation.
  • All groups and individuals are welcome to enter this category.

Craftsmanship

  • This is strictly for contestants who created their own costumes and want to have their technical skill and construction methods formally evaluated.
  • Costumes entered will be judged based on the quality, technique, and complexity of the costume's construction.
  • Any contestants who do not wish to walk the runway will forfeit any potential of winning the "Best in Show" award.
  • Any contestants who enter the Craftsmanship Category, but fail to show for pre-judging will forfeit any potential of winning any Craftsmanship Only awards.

Tournament Arc — Craftsmanship Divisions & Entry Criteria

For fair competition, contestants are divided by experience and previous awards. Contestants may choose to enter a higher division but may not enter a division below their qualification. Judges may move a contestant to a higher division based on their collective judgment of the costume's craftsmanship. For the Tournament Arc contest only, contestants will be judged according to the criteria defined for their division (Novice, Journeyman, Master).

Novice

  • Intended for contestants new to costume making and competition.
  • Contestant has won no more than one major craftsmanship award at any convention.
  • At least 25% of the costume must be hand‑made by the contestant.

Journeyman

  • For contestants with some experience in costume making and competition.
  • Contestant has won more than one but fewer than three major craftsmanship awards in total.
  • At least 50% of the costume must be hand‑made by the contestant.

Master

  • For highly experienced crafters and professional costume makers.
  • Contestant has won three or more major awards or has professional experience.
  • At least 75% of the costume must be hand‑made by the contestant.

Winning Categories

Beach Episode Cosplay Contest

  • Best in Show (Highest overall score — Craftsmanship category only)
  • Craftsmanship (Highest craftsmanship score — Craftsmanship category only)
  • Best Group (Runway category only)
  • Best in Character (Open to all contestants)
  • Best Wig (Open to all contestants)
  • Judges' Choice (Personal award from each judge — open to all contestants)

Tournament Arc Cosplay Contest

  • Best Novice (Highest novice-division craftsmanship score — Craftsmanship category only)
  • Best Journeyman (Highest journeyman-division craftsmanship score — Craftsmanship category only)
  • Best Master (Highest master-division craftsmanship score — Craftsmanship category only)
  • Best in Show (Highest overall score — Craftsmanship category only)
  • Best Group (Runway category only)
  • Judges' Choice (Personal award from each judge — open to all contestants)
