The Ship
Tips: Bring Your Own Drinks & Food
January 27, 2024
Can I bring my own Alcohol onboard to drink?
On embarkation day, each guest of drinking age may bring one (1) sealed 750 ml bottle of wine or champagne. Boxed wine and other containers are prohibited. Guests who purchase alcohol bottles onboard, in a port-of-call, or bring more than the one permitted bottle on boarding day, will have their items safely stored by the ship. These bottles will be returned on the last day onboard for enjoyment once home.
NOTE: Cruisers under the age of 21 cannot bring alcohol on board regardless, and underage individuals attempting to bring alcohol on board will have their alcohol confiscated and they will be denied boarding.
Alcoholic beverages (beer, seltzer or hard liquor) seized on embarkation day will not be returned.
What if I buy Alcohol at a port?
Yes. However, alcohol bought at ports will be stored until the last night of the cruise.
What if I buy alcohol in the shops on the boat?
All liquor bottles purchased at the onboard shops cannot be taken to your stateroom. It will be stored during the duration of the cruise and then distributed on the last night of the voyage.
What about non-alcoholic beverages?
Guests may also bring non-alcoholic beverages as carry-on items on boarding day. Non-alcoholic beverages may not exceed 12 standard (17 oz.) cans, bottles or cartons per stateroom. Milk and distilled water brought on for infant, medical, or dietary use are permitted.
